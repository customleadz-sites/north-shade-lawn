Should I Choose Pavers or Concrete for My Patio?

It's the first question almost every homeowner asks us: should I build my patio with pavers or concrete? Both will give you a flat outdoor surface, but they age very differently — especially in Michigan, where the freeze-and-thaw cycle is brutal on poured concrete. For the vast majority of patios we build, the answer is pavers, and here's why.

The short answer: pavers

A paver patio is built from individual concrete or stone units set on a deep, compacted base. A concrete patio is one large poured slab. That single difference — many small pieces versus one big slab — is exactly why a paver patio holds up better, looks better, and is easier to live with over the long run.

Pavers beat concrete on Michigan's freeze-thaw

Poured concrete cracks. It's not a question of if but when. As the ground freezes and thaws through a mid-Michigan winter, a solid concrete slab has nowhere to move, so it heaves and develops cracks you can't really fix. Pavers flex with the ground. The joints between each paver absorb that movement, so a properly installed paver patio stays level and crack-free season after season.

A concrete slab is only as good as the day it's poured. A paver patio can be lifted, re-leveled and reset — which is why pavers keep looking new for decades.

Repairs: pavers win, easily

If a section of a paver patio ever settles or a single paver gets stained, we lift those pavers, fix the base, and reset them — no patchwork, no mismatched color. With a cracked concrete patio, your options are to live with it or tear out and re-pour the whole slab. For repairability alone, pavers are the smarter long-term choice.

Looks and resale value

Pavers come in a huge range of colors, shapes and laying patterns — herringbone, running bond, contrasting borders — so your patio can match your home instead of looking like a gray pad. That custom, high-end look is also why a quality paver patio tends to add more resale value than plain concrete. Buyers notice the difference the moment they walk into the backyard.

What about cost?

Concrete can be cheaper up front, and that's the one honest argument in its favor. But once you factor in cracking, repairs and replacement down the road, a well-built paver patio usually wins on value over its lifetime. You're paying a bit more once instead of paying to patch or replace a slab later.

  • Paver patios flex with freeze-thaw — no cracking
  • Individual pavers can be lifted, re-leveled and reset
  • Far more colors, patterns and custom borders than concrete
  • Stronger curb appeal and resale value
