Your Fall Cleanup Checklist for a Healthier Spring Lawn

Here's the thing most people miss about a great spring lawn: it's made in the fall. The work you do — or skip — in October and November decides how green and thick things come back in April. Run through this checklist before the snow flies and you'll be ahead of every neighbor on the block.

1. Clear the leaves (all of them)

A light dusting of leaves is fine. A thick, wet mat is not — it blocks sunlight, traps moisture and invites mold and disease that can leave dead patches by spring. Get leaves and debris off the lawn and hauled away, not just blown into a corner.

2. Keep mowing — but lower the last cuts

Don't stop mowing too early. Gradually drop your cutting height on the final mows of the season. A shorter final cut helps prevent matting and snow mold over winter, without scalping the crown of the grass.

3. Aerate and overseed

Fall is the best window to aerate and overseed in Michigan. Aeration relieves compaction and lets air, water and nutrients reach the roots; overseeding thickens thin spots while soil is still warm enough to germinate. This one step does more for next year's lawn than almost anything else.

Compacted soil is the silent killer of tired lawns. If yours feels hard underfoot and water runs off instead of soaking in, aeration is your fix.

4. Cut back and tidy the beds

Trim back spent perennials and overgrown shrubs, pull the last weeds, and refresh bed edges. Going into winter clean means a far faster, cheaper start in spring.

5. The fall cleanup checklist

  • Leaves & debris removed and hauled away
  • Final mows stepped down in height
  • Lawn aerated and overseeded where needed
  • Perennials and shrubs cut back
  • Bed edges refreshed and weeds pulled
  • Gutters and walkways cleared of buildup
