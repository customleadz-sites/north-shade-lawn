What's the Best Material for a Walkway?

A walkway is the first thing guests step onto and the path you use every single day, so it pays to choose the right material. The three most common options are pavers, natural stone and concrete — and for most mid-Michigan homes, a paver walkway is the best all-around choice. Here's how they compare.

Paver walkways — the best all-rounder

A paver walkway gives you the most flexibility and the longest worry-free life. Pavers come in endless colors and patterns, they flex with Michigan's freeze-and-thaw instead of cracking, and if one ever stains or settles, we lift it and reset it. That repairability is a big reason paver walkways stay looking sharp for decades.

The best walkway material is the one that still looks great after ten winters — and that's usually pavers, because the joints let the path move without cracking.

Natural stone — high-end and organic

A natural stone walkway (flagstone or cut stone) brings a timeless, custom look that's hard to beat. It's typically the premium option, both in material cost and in the craft it takes to set each piece. If you want a one-of-a-kind garden path with real character, natural stone is worth a look.

Concrete — budget-friendly but prone to cracking

Poured concrete is the lowest up-front cost, but a single slab walkway is the most likely to crack and heave over Michigan winters, and a cracked concrete path is hard to repair invisibly. It can make sense for a simple, straight run on a budget, but it rarely offers the looks or longevity of pavers.

So, what's the best walkway material?

For durability, design flexibility and easy repairs, a paver walkway is the best material for most homes. Choose natural stone if you want a premium, organic look and have the budget; choose concrete only when up-front cost is the deciding factor.

  • Pavers — best overall: durable, flexible, repairable
  • Natural stone — premium, timeless, custom
  • Concrete — cheapest up front, most prone to cracking
